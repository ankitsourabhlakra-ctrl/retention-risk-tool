How to use this tool

Single School Analysis

1
Fill in the metrics form
Enter values for all 10 metrics on the left panel. Every field has a hint showing what to enter and the valid range. Leave fields at 0 if data is unavailable.
2
Click Analyse School Risk
The tool instantly calculates a risk score using domain-weighted scoring. No AI call is needed for the score — it runs on your browser.
3
Read the risk card
You see the score (0-100), tier (Red/Amber/Green), any red flags triggered, and a metric breakdown bar chart showing what is driving the score.
4
Read the AI narrative
Claude writes a 3-sentence narrative: current situation, root cause, and one specific action for the field team this week.

Batch CSV Analysis

1
Download the sample CSV
Click "Download Sample CSV" on the Batch Upload tab to get a template with the correct column headers and 10 example schools pre-filled.
2
Fill in your school data
Replace the sample rows with your real school data. Keep the column headers exactly as they are. Save as .csv format.
3
Upload the CSV
Drag and drop the file or click to browse. All schools are scored instantly — no API calls needed for scoring, so it works on 100 schools in seconds.
4
Filter, sort, and deep dive
Filter by Red/Amber/Green tier. Sort by highest risk, most red flags, or school name. Click any row to generate an AI deep-dive narrative for that school.
Risk Tiers Explained
● Red — High Risk
Score ≥ 65 OR any red flag
Immediate intervention required. Field visit within 48-72 hours. Account likely to churn without action.
● Amber — Watch
Score 35–64, no red flags
Early warning signals present. Schedule a proactive check-in within 2 weeks. Monitor closely at renewal.
● Green — Healthy
Score < 35, no red flags
School is engaged and on track. Maintain regular cadence. Good candidate for upsell or referral.

Frequently Asked Questions
What if I don't have data for some fields?
Enter 0 for numeric fields you don't have. For NPS previous, enter the same value as NPS latest. The model will still give a useful score — just less precise.
How is the score calculated?
Each metric is normalised to a 0–100 risk score then multiplied by its weight. The weighted scores are summed. Red flags override the tier to Red regardless of the score.
Why does a Green score sometimes show Red?
Red flags are independent of the score. A school can score 25 (Green) but still be forced to Red if, say, no training has happened in 90 days. The flag overrides the tier.
Can I change the weights?
The weights are currently set based on domain expertise from EdTech operations experience. Future versions will support custom weight configuration and regression-based tuning.
